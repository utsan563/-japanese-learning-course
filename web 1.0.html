Introduction

Welcome to the Japanese Learning Course! This course is designed to help you get started with the basics of the Japanese language. Whether you're studying for the JLPT N5 exam or just want to learn some useful phrases, this course will guide you through essential vocabulary and grammar.

Kanji Learning

Kanji are the characters used in Japanese writing that originate from Chinese characters. Learning kanji is essential for reading and writing in Japanese. Here are some basic kanji to get you started:

Numbers

  • 一 (いち, one)
  • 二 (に, two)
  • 三 (さん, three)
  • 四 (し/よん, four)
  • 五 (ご, five)
  • 六 (ろく, six)
  • 七 (しち/なな, seven)
  • 八 (はち, eight)
  • 九 (きゅう/く, nine)
  • 十 (じゅう, ten)

Days of the Week

  • 日 (にち/ひ, day/sun) - 日曜日 (にちようび, Sunday)
  • 月 (げつ/つき, moon/month) - 月曜日 (げつようび, Monday)
  • 火 (か/ひ, fire) - 火曜日 (かようび, Tuesday)
  • 水 (すい/みず, water) - 水曜日 (すいようび, Wednesday)
  • 木 (もく/き, tree) - 木曜日 (もくようび, Thursday)
  • 金 (きん/かね, gold/money) - 金曜日 (きんようび, Friday)
  • 土 (ど/つち, earth) - 土曜日 (どようび, Saturday)

Common Verbs

  • 食 (しょく/たべる, eat) - 食べる (たべる, to eat)
  • 飲 (いん/のむ, drink) - 飲む (のむ, to drink)
  • 行 (こう/いく, go) - 行く (いく, to go)
  • 見 (けん/みる, see) - 見る (みる, to see/watch)
  • 来 (らい/くる, come) - 来る (くる, to come)
  • 話 (わ/はなす, speak) - 話す (はなす, to speak)
  • 書 (しょ/かく, write) - 書く (かく, to write)

Japanese Sentences

Here are some useful Japanese sentences for everyday use:

  • お元気ですか？ (おげんきですか？) - How are you?
  • 私は学生です。 (わたしはがくせいです。) - I am a student.
  • これは何ですか？ (これはなんですか？) - What is this?
  • どこに行きますか？ (どこにいきますか？) - Where are you going?
  • すみません、遅れました。 (すみません、おくれました。) - Sorry, I'm late.
  • 助けてください！ (たすけてください！) - Please help me!
  • これはおいしいです。 (これはおいしいです。) - This is delicious.
  • どれくらいかかりますか？ (どれくらいかかりますか？) - How long will it take?
  • 私は日本語を勉強しています。 (わたしはにほんごをべんきょうしています。) - I am studying Japanese.
  • お会いできてうれしいです。 (おあいできてうれしいです。) - Nice to meet you.

Japanese Practical Examples

Here are some practical examples with detailed explanations and translations:

Example 1: Introducing Yourself

こんにちは、私の名前は[Your Name]です。よろしくお願いします。

Hello, my name is [Your Name]. Nice to meet you.

Pronunciation: Konnichiwa, watashi no namae wa [Your Name] desu. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.

Explanation: Use this introduction when meeting someone for the first time. "よろしくお願いします" is a polite way to express "Nice to meet you" or "Please take care of me."

Example 2: Asking for Directions

すみません、駅はどこですか？

Excuse me, where is the train station?

Pronunciation: Sumimasen, eki wa doko desu ka?

Explanation: Use this phrase when you need to ask for directions to a place. "すみません" is a polite way to get someone's attention.

Example 3: Ordering Food

この料理は辛いですか？

Is this dish spicy?

Pronunciation: Kono ryouri wa karai desu ka?

Explanation: Use this question when you want to know if a dish is spicy before ordering it. "この料理" refers to "this dish" or "this food."

Example 4: Making a Request

このレポートを明日までに提出してください。

Please submit this report by tomorrow.

Pronunciation: Kono repōto o ashita made ni teishutsu shite kudasai.

Explanation: Use this phrase to make a polite request for something to be done by a specific time. "提出してください" is a polite way to say "please submit."

Example 5: Shopping for Clothes

このシャツは試着できますか？

Can I try on this shirt?

Pronunciation: Kono shatsu wa shichaku dekimasu ka?

Explanation: Use this phrase when you want to try on clothes in a store. "試着" means "trying on" and "できますか" means "can I?"

Example 6: Asking for the Price

これの値段はいくらですか？

How much is this?

Pronunciation: Kore no nedan wa ikura desu ka?

Explanation: Use this phrase when you want to ask the price of an item. "値段" means "price" and "いくら" means "how much."

Example 7: Making a Reservation

テーブルを予約したいのですが。

I would like to make a reservation for a table.

Pronunciation: Tēburu o yoyaku shitai no desu ga.

Explanation: Use this phrase when you want to reserve a table at a restaurant. "予約" means "reservation" and "したい" means "want to do."

Example 8: Asking for Help

手伝ってもらえますか？

Can you help me?

Pronunciation: Tetsudatte moraemasu ka?

Explanation: Use this phrase to ask someone for help. "手伝って" means "help" and "もらえますか" means "can you?"

Study Tips

  • Practice regularly: Consistency is key to mastering Japanese.
  • Use flashcards: Tools like Anki can help with memorizing vocabulary.
  • Engage with native material: Watch Japanese TV shows, listen to Japanese music, and read Japanese books to immerse yourself in the language.
  • Join a language exchange: Practicing with native speakers can significantly improve your speaking skills.
  • Set achievable goals: Break down your learning into manageable chunks and celebrate your progress.
